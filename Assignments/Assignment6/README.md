# Assignment 6: Exploring Non-Traditional Health Data

## 📌 Overview
This assignment explores non-traditional health data and its role in improving healthcare outcomes, with a focus on Social Determinants of Health (SDOH) and genetic testing data.

The project examines how these data sources extend beyond traditional clinical information and contribute to predictive analytics, personalized medicine, and population health management. It also evaluates important ethical concerns such as privacy, bias, and data misuse.

## 🎯 Objectives
- Understand non-traditional health data sources  
- Analyze the impact of SDOH on health outcomes  
- Explore genetic testing and its role in precision medicine  
- Evaluate ethical and privacy challenges in healthcare data  

## 🧠 Learning Process
In this assignment, I studied two major types of non-traditional health data:

### 1. Social Determinants of Health (SDOH)
- Income level  
- Education  
- Living environment  

### 2. Genetic Testing Data
- DNA-based health risk analysis  
- Pharmacogenomics (drug response prediction)  

The learning process involved:
- Analyzing real-world research linking SDOH to health outcomes  
- Understanding how genetic testing services (e.g., 23andMe) work  
- Evaluating how combining social and biological data improves predictions  
- Identifying ethical risks associated with sensitive data  

This helped me understand how healthcare AI goes beyond medical records and includes broader life context.

## 🚀 Implementation (Conceptual Analysis)
The assignment evaluates two key non-traditional data sources:

### 🔹 Social Determinants of Health (SDOH)
- Provide insight into social and environmental influences  
- Help identify at-risk populations  
- Improve population-level predictions  

### 🔹 Genetic Testing Data
- Identify inherited disease risks  
- Support personalized treatment decisions  
- Enable pharmacogenomics applications  

### 🔹 Combined Use in AI
- Enhances prediction accuracy  
- Provides a more holistic view of patient health  
- Supports preventive and personalized care strategies  

## 📊 Results & Outcomes
- Demonstrated that SDOH significantly influence health outcomes  
- Showed how genetic data enables personalized medicine  
- Identified that combining both data types improves predictive models  
- Highlighted the growing importance of **non-clinical data in AI systems**  

## ⚖️ Ethical & Privacy Considerations
- Data privacy risks (especially genetic data)  
- Potential discrimination (insurance, employment)  
- Algorithmic bias from social data  
- Risk of reinforcing health inequalities  
- Need for transparency and informed consent  

## 💡 Key Takeaways
- Healthcare AI must consider both **biological and social factors**  
- Non-traditional data improves prediction but increases ethical complexity  
- SDOH has strong population-level impact, while genetic data supports individual-level care  
- Responsible data governance is critical in healthcare AI
