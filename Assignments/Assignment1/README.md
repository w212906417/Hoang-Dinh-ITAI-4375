# Assignment 1: Medical Terminology in AI-Driven Healthcare

## 📌 Overview
This module introduces foundational medical terminology essential for understanding how Artificial Intelligence (AI) is applied in healthcare. The assignment focuses on identifying, defining, and contextualizing key clinical and technological terms from recent medical literature (2025 studies).

The goal is to bridge the gap between medical knowledge and AI applications by understanding how these terms are used in real-world research and clinical settings.

## 🎯 Objectives
- Develop familiarity with advanced medical terminology
- Understand how AI is integrated into modern healthcare systems
- Interpret clinical research language in peer-reviewed studies
- Build a foundation for future AI healthcare applications

## 🧠 Learning Process
In this module, I analyzed recent peer-reviewed medical studies and extracted 10 important terms related to:
- Neuroimmune interactions
- AI-based diagnostics
- Medical imaging techniques
- Epidemiological research methods
- Digital health systems

Each term was:
1. Defined in simple, clear language  
2. Supported with real research context  
3. Evaluated for its importance in healthcare and AI applications  

This process helped me move beyond memorization and toward **applied understanding**, especially in interpreting how AI contributes to diagnosis, treatment, and research.

## ⚙️ Technologies & Tools
- Academic databases (Nature Medicine, BMJ Open, PubMed Central)
- AI-assisted research support tools
- Scientific literature analysis

## 🚀 Implementation
The assignment includes 10 key medical and AI-related terms:

- Neuroimmune Modulation  
- Vagus Nerve Stimulation (VNS)  
- Metabolic Dysfunction-Associated Steatotic Liver Disease (MASLD)  
- Propensity Score Matching  
- AI-Enabled Electrocardiogram (DULCE)  
- Chronic Liver Disease (CLD)  
- Diffuse Midline Glioma  
- Diffusion Kurtosis Imaging (DKI)  
- Digital Health Technologies (DHTs)  
- Rare Diseases  

Each term was analyzed with:
- Definition  
- Real-world research context  
- Clinical and technological significance  

## 📊 Results & Outcomes
- Gained strong understanding of **medical terminology used in AI healthcare research**
- Improved ability to read and interpret **peer-reviewed medical papers**
- Learned how AI is applied in:
  - Disease detection (e.g., AI-enabled ECG)
  - Imaging analysis (e.g., MRI-based techniques)
  - Clinical trials (e.g., digital health technologies)

## 💡 Key Takeaways
- AI in healthcare is deeply connected to domain-specific medical knowledge  
- Understanding terminology is critical for building accurate AI models  
- Many modern healthcare innovations combine:
  - Medical devices  
  - Machine learning  
  - Clinical data analysis
